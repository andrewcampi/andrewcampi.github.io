Checkout Simulation

Objective:
To use the priority queue abstract data type and event driven simulation.
Overview:

In a store, there are a set amount of cashiers ready to serve customers that arrive in line. As an owner of a store, it is very important to set this amount correctly. If the amount of cashiers is set to high, money will be wasted by paying for idle cashiers. If the amount of cashiers is set too low, customers will become unhappy because they wait in line too long. A simulation can be constructed with variables so that this balance can be determined for any situation.

Queue:
A queue is a list of objects in a specific order. For this example, the objects in the lists are customers in a line. Initially the line is empty. The first customer arrives in the line, that customer will be served. However, when many customers are on the line and another customer arrives, the customer that just arrived will be served after every customer in the line ahead is served. In a queue, this is called an enqueue event, when an object is added to the back of the queue. When a cashier is ready to serve a customer, the customer that has been on the line the longest is taken off the line and matched with the cashier that is ready to serve the customer. In a queue, this is called a dequeue event, when an object is removed from the front of the queue.
Priority Queue:

A priority queue is a queue with slightly different enqueue and dequeue functions. For this example, the priority queue is a time-line and the objects in the priority queue are events on the time-line. New events on a time-line can be added to any spot in the timeline (i.e. in front of events that were already on the time-line). Priority queue objects store a priority number. While it might seem backwards, priority number zero is the most important priority number, and any event with priority number zero will happen the soonest. Objects with larger priority numbers are less important and will happen later. For this example, the priority number is the time of occurrence. Time zero (priority number zero) is the instant the store opens. There is not always a largest priority number, but for this example, the largest priority number is a variable that represents the closing time of the store.

The Technical Details:
  • In this simulation, there are two sets of “cashiers”: cashiers in the actual store, and gift-wrappers who gift-wrap completed orders. Customers need to come back to the store after they drop off their completed order to be gift-wrapped, so we will call the gift-wrappers “pickup-helpers”.
  • There are “n” number of cashiers.
  • There are “m” number of pickup-helpers.
  • There are three events that occur for a regular customer: “Customer Arrival Event”, “Customer Begin Service Event”, and “Customer End Service Event”.
  • There is a “p” percent chance that a customer will want their completed order gift-wrapped.
  • For customers who want their completed order gift-wrapped, there are three events that occur: “Pickup Arrival Event”, “Pickup Begin Service Event”, and “Customer End Service Event”.
  • All six events occur in one “event queue” (time-line).
Customer Arrival Event:
  • A customer arrives at the cashier line at a random time.
  • If a cashier is idle (not serving any customers), the customer is immediately taken off the line and paired with the idle cashier. That customer waited in line for 0 time.
  • If no cashier is free, the customer joins the back of the line.
  • A customer arrives during open hours at a random time 1 to lambda, where lambda is the specified maximum inter-arrival time integer.
Cashier Service Begin Event:
  • The customer starts to receive service from the cashier they are paired to.
  • The cashier’s state is changed from “idle” to “busy”.
  • The amount of time the customer spends with the cashier is a random amount of time 1 to mu, where mu is the specified maximum service time.
  • When this event takes place, a Cashier Service End Event should be scheduled in the event queue.
  • When the customer starts to receive service, their time spent on line should be computed and recorded.
Cashier Service End Event:
  • When a customer finishes the service with the cashier, the simulation should increment the total number of customers who finished service.
  • The cashier’s state is changed from “busy” to “idle” (the cashier is now ready to serve the next customer in line if the line is not empty).
  • A random number in the range of 1 to 100 is generated.
  • If the random number is less than or equal to the percent chance of the customer needing gift-wrapping, schedule a Pickup Arrival Event at the current time of the simulation.
Pickup Arrival Event:
  • A customer arrives at the pickup helper line.
  • If a pickup helper is idle (not serving any customers), the customer is immediately taken off the line and paired with the idle pickup helper. That customer waited in the pickup line for 0 time.
  • If no pickup helper is free, the customer joins the back of the pickup helper line.
Pickup Service Begin Event:
  • The customer starts to receive service from the pickup helper they are paired to.
  • The pickup helper’s state is changed from “idle” to “busy”.
  • The amount of time the gift takes to be wrapped is a random amount of time 1 to mu, where mu is the specified maximum service time.
  • When this event takes place, a Pickup Service End Event should be scheduled in the event queue.
  • When the customer starts to receive service, their time spent on the pickup line should be computed and recorded.
Pickup Service End Event:
  • When the pickup helper is done wrapping the gift, the simulation should increment the total number of customers who finished getting their gift wrapped.
  • The pickup helper’s state is changed from “busy” to “idle” (the pickup helper is now ready to serve the next customer in the pickup line if the line is not empty).
Alterable Variables:
  • “n” number of cashiers.
  • “m” number of pickup helpers.
  • “lambda” amount of time between the next customer arriving.
  • “mu” maximum individual service time.
  • Largest priority value in the event queue (The closing time).
  • “p” percent chance that a customer will want their completed order gift wrapped.
Recorded Statistics:
  • Number of customers arrived
  • Number of customers served
  • Number of customers remaining in line at closing time.
  • Average wait time.
  • Number of pickup customers arrived.
  • Number of pickup customers served.
  • Average wait time for pickup customers.
