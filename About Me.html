About Me

Bio

I would like to take the time to introduce myself in detail. My name is Andrew Campi and I am a Junior student at Monmouth University. I spent my first and second years of college at Bucknell University in Pennsylvania. I studied Computer Science and did very well academically at Bucknell. In addition to receiving a libral arts education, I learned how to program in Python, Java, C, Matlab, and Assembly. While the education was strong, I did not feel connected to the community and wanted to live at home for a change. So, I made the decision to transfer to Monmouth University. I have been enjoying the classes so far. I am further developing my main focus of Computer Science, Cyber Security. 

To practice “ethical hacking”, I have created a dozen different “vulnerable machines”. These machines are virtualized, and are created with a small hidden hole in their security which is known as a critical vulnerability. Exploiting these vulnerabilities allows the hacker to have full access to the target machine. The task is to find these vulnerabilities and exploit them using various real world hacking tools.

In my free time, I enjoy spending time with my family, playing with my dog, and game development. I develop 3D games using the Unity game engine. Scripts for these games are written in a language called C#. My favorite part of game development is level design, in which I get to build game worlds to make them fun, functional, and visually appealing. I am still definitely a beginner, and like most  hobbyists, I rely heavily on internet resources like YouTube tutorials when I get stuck. 

Thanks for reading!
