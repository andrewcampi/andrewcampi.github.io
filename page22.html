Theatre Seats

Logic Puzzle

Overview:

There is a special, sold-out showing of Bong Joon-ho's moive "Parasite" at the local theatre. For this speical showing, the seats have been numbered and viewers have reserved particular seats (so your ticket tells you which seat you are to sit in). The local theatre has 310 seats. The first person who enters the theatre somehow spilled soda all over their ticket as they walked in and it is now illegible - they have no idea which seat they had reserved. So he randomly chooses a seat and hopes for the best. Each other person that comes in either sits in their assigned seat or, if it is already taken, they randomly choose an unoccupied seat.

Question:
When the 310th person walks in, what is the probability their seat will be unoccupied?
The Answer:

The probability that the first person sits in his actual seat is 1/n. The probability that he sits in the 310ths peron's seat is also 1/n. Start the formula with p(n) = (1/n)(1)+(1/n)(0). 

Now, the rest of the formula cane be expressed by ((n-2)/n)(p(n-1)) because ((n-2)/n) is the percentage of seats left and p(n-1) is the probability of the first person not sitting in his seat or the 310th person's seat. We can assume that this boils down to 50/50 chance of the 309th person selecting either the 310th person's seat or the other remaining unoccupied seat. 

Thus, p(n) = (1/n)(1) + ((n-2)/n)(p(n-1)) + (1/n)(0)

p(310) = 50%
