The Island of Zombies

Logic Puzzle

Overview:

On a certian island, some of the residents have been turned to zombies, but they act just like normal people except that they only lie. The humans on the island only tell the truth.
The islanders understand English, but they only speak in their native tongue which has not yet been translated. When you ask them a yes or no question, they respond with "gha" or "jal." One means yes, and the other no, but you don't know which.
You arrive on the island and are immediately captured. The leader of the island points to a card written in English above his head. It says: "You may ask the leader any one question. If he answers "gha" then you may visit the island peacefully, if he answers "jal" then you will be transformed into a zombie and forced to live out your days on the island.

Question:
What do you ask the leader, given that you don't know whether he is a human or a zombie and given that you would prefer not to be a zombie?
The Answer:

There are four different scenarios that could occur when the leader is required to respond "yes" or "no". Each scenario is created by a different question:

#1 : Are you a human?

#2: Are you a zombie?

#3: Was I captured?

#4: Am I on a boat?

Question #1: If the leader is human, the answer will be "yes". If the leader is a zombie, the answer will be "yes". Therefore, the only answer the leader can give to this question is "yes".

Question #2: If the leader is human, the answer will be "no". If the leader is a zombie, the answer will be "no". Therefore, the only answer the leader can give to this question is "no".

Question #3: If the leader is human, the answer will be "yes".  If the leader is a zombie, the answer will be "no."

Question #4: If the leader is human, the answer will be "no". If the leader is a zombie, the answer will be "yes".

However, it does not matter which question you ask the leader, because the probability of you surviving ultimately depends on the translation of "gha" and "jal". There is a 50% chance that "yes" translates to "gha", and a 50% chance that "no" translates to "gha". Therefore, it does not matter if the leader responds "yes or "no", meaning that it does not matter if the leader is a human or a zombie, ultimately meaning that it does not matter what question you ask. For every scenario, there is a 50% chance that the answer provided ("yes" or "no") translates to the word ("gha") that permits you to stay on the island peacefully.
